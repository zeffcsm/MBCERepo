What is Blockchain?

Blockchain can be seen as a ledger which keeps transactions and records linked by using a concept in cryptography called hashing. It is a growing list of records that can be seen by anyone concerned in order to verify, approve and cross check transactions or their respective statuses. The word Block in Blockchain refers to the information that make up a block and the word Chain refers to the chain linked structure that the Blockchain holds.

Why Blockchain?

The internet has revolutionized the way we approach commerce, communications, financial services, entertainment, intellectual property and so much more. Blockchain technology is the Internet’s missing piece, allowing for any asset to be digitized, transferred and recorded on transparent and distributed ledgers that offer real security. Now anyone with an internet connection can transfer assets directly on a system with unmatched security, integrity and efficiency. No more paper and no more slow, expensive and hackable intermediaries.

Key Advantages

Transparency & Traceability

Blockchain technology consists of open ledgers for anyone to see transactions undertaken given that they are involved in it. This enables all parties to check the transaction states and retrace every single step that has been taken from the starting point which makes gives blockchain the trustworthy and fail-proof characteristics. Each user is also responsible of signing their own transactions without the need of a third party to monitor them.

Decentralized Network

The strength of Blockchain technology lies in its decentralised network of computers by having a copy of the ledgers in each machine, making it an unhackable concept while also eliminating any attempt of monopoly of the technology since it has no central regulator. It also implies that blockchain does not have a single point of failure which makes it more robust than a traditional server type architecture.

Validation & Verification

Blockchain makes validation of previous records/transaction happen at a glance, drastically improving waiting times of authentication or confirmation for any type of information as long as the data is part of the blockchain. Records cannot be tampered or modified without a consensus and therefore verification of identity or certificates become a much easier to process. This aspect of blockchain also removes the need of a third party to certify any documents required and will dramatically change costs involved when needing these intermediaries

Multi Sectorial Use Cases

Blockchain technology aspires to be the underlying technology behind multiple systems where trust is required as a form of confirmation or verification. Sectors part of the array that would highly benefit from blockchain include but are not limited to ; Banking & Finance, Agriculture, Energy Distribution, Insurance and Real Estate. More details on practical use cases of blockchain in the aforementioned areas are found below.

Areas of focus

Agro Industry

With Blockchain transparency and traceability, prices of goods between farmers and buyers will be fully disclosed , preventing all risks of scams or selling at a much higher price.

Banking & Finance

With the ability of verifying records and past transactions much faster without the need of a third party, cross-border payments will be easier and less costly to make.

Supply Chain Management

Tracking of shipments can be done at a glance with the high traceability of Blockchain ledgers, while enabling a much faster transfer of goods from one party to another with a highly secured verification process.

Insurance

Identity management in Blockchain is highly secured and trustworthy, setting up a gateway for insurance services to thrive and transition their processes on the Blockchain.

Real Estate

Contracts and leases can be easily transfered from one person to another in a very secured environment where real estate listings can also be posted for sale or rent.
